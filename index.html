Hiragana - the foundation of Japanese

Hiragana is the first script you'll learn. It has 46 basic characters, each representing one sound. Click any character to learn it.

Katakana - for foreign words and emphasis

Katakana has the same sounds as hiragana, but with sharper strokes. It is mainly used for loanwords, foreign names, and scientific terms.

46 charactersAngular, sharp strokesLoanwords and foreign names

Dakuten and Handakuten

+25 voiced sounds
Dakuten (゛) adds voicing: か (ka) becomes が (ga). Handakuten (゜) turns the H row into the P row.

Combination Kana

33 compound sounds
Combine an I-row kana with small や, ゆ, or よ to create sounds like きゃ, きゅ, きょ.
