Hiragana — the foundation of Japanese

Hiragana is the first script you'll learn. It has 46 basic characters, each representing a sound (syllable). Once you know hiragana, you can read most Japanese texts with furigana and many beginner materials. Click any character to learn it.

46 characters Curved, flowing strokes Used for native Japanese words Verb endings · Particles
ア

Katakana — for foreign words & emphasis

Katakana has the same 46 sounds as hiragana but with angular, sharper strokes. It's mainly used for loanwords (コーヒー = coffee, テレビ = TV), foreign names, and scientific terms. Click any character to learn it.

46 characters Angular, sharp strokes Loanwords · Foreign names Sound effects · Emphasis

Dakuten & Handakuten

+25 voiced sounds
💡 Dakuten (゛) adds a voiced sound — か (ka) → が (ga). Handakuten (゜) turns H-row into P-row — は (ha) → ぱ (pa).

Combination Kana

33 new sounds
💡 Combine an I-row character with small や・ゆ・よ to create compound sounds. The small kana is written smaller and the two characters together make one sound: きゃ = "kya".
